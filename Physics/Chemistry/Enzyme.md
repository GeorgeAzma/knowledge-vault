**Biological catalyst** speeds up chemical reactions

Almost every chemical reaction in a living cell would happen _way too slowly_ on its own to support life. 
Enzymes solve this by lowering the **activation energy** — the energy barrier a reaction needs to get started

- **enzymes are almost always proteins** but can also be [[RNA]]
- enzyme typically works on a single molecule called **substrate**, or single type of reaction
- **reusable** after the reaction, the enzyme is released unchanged and can do it again

Enzymes have an **active site** — a pocket or groove where the substrate binds. Two models describe this:
- **Lock and key** substrate fits the active site perfectly, like a key into a lock
- **Induced fit** `more accurate` the enzyme _flexes and adjusts_ its shape slightly when the substrate binds
