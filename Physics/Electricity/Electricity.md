### Intuition
Imagine electricity as water
- [[Charge]] $Q$ is water amount `liters`
- [[Current]] $I$ is flow `liters/second` or water velocity if we assume 1 liter of water
- [[Voltage]] $V$ is how much pressure you apply to push the water `initial acceleration`
- [[Resistance]] is narrower pipe, harder to push water through
- $I=\dfrac{V}{R}$ water moves depending on how hard you pushed, divided by how narrow the pipe is
- [[Power]] $W=IV=I^2R=\dfrac{V^2}{R}$ rate at which water does work
     how much water would keep pushing you back if it was directed at you `or paddle wheel`
    - $\dfrac{V^2}{R}$ pressure squared $V^2$ divided by resistance
    - $IV$ flow multiplied by pressure `lots of water flowing with high pressure`
    - $\dfrac{V^2}{R}$ pressure squared divided by resistance
- [[Capacitance]] $Q=CV$ more capacitance the ballon has and more pressure it can hold, more charge it can hold
- [[Inductance]] $V=L\dfrac{dI}{dt}$ resists change in flow `inertia`, faster you try to increase the flow $\dfrac{dI}{dt}$
   more it resists depending on how much inductance $L$ you have
