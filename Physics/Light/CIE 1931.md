### Experiment
- shine all wavelengths of test [[Light|lights]] from `380nm` to`700nm` with `5-10nm` step size
- have 3 primary colors `R, G, B = ~(700nm, 546nm, 435nm)`
- make human observers `~17` adjust `RGB` to match test lights
    - `R,G,B` can be negative, where primaries are added to test light instead
    - measures LMS cone response in central $2\degree$ FOV `fovea`
     `where cones are most densely packed`
- this gives averaged responses $\bar{r}(\lambda),\bar{g}(\lambda),\bar{b}(\lambda)$ called **Color Matching Functions (CMFs)**
- get **Normalized CMF** by normalizing area under curve $\int_0^\infty \bar{r}(\lambda)d\lambda=\int_0^\infty \bar{g}(\lambda)d\lambda=\int_0^\infty \bar{b}(\lambda)d\lambda$
- scale normalized CMF by `1, 4.5907, 0.0601` to get source luminance
- scale normalized CMF by `72.0962, 1.3791, 1` to get source radiance
