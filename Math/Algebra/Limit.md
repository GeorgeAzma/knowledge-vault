### Formula $e=\lim_{x\to\infty}{(1+\frac{1}{x})^x}$
### [[Simplifications]]
- For $\infty,-\infty$ limits only terms with highest growth rate matter
  $O(1)<O(\log(n))<O(\sqrt n)<O(n)<O(n\log(n))<O(n^2)<O(\exp(n))<O(n!)<O(n^n)$
	- higher degree terms dominate $\lim_{x\to\infty}\large\frac{x^2+x^3}{2x^3}=\frac{1}{2}$
	- exponentials dominate over [[Polynomial]][[s]] $\lim_{x\to\infty}\large\frac{e^x}{x^9}=\infty$
